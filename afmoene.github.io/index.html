Field work in times of Covid-19

Field Practical Hydrology, Water Quality and Meteorology - Wageningen University

In the course 'Field Practical Hydrology, Water Quality and Meteorology' students normally spend about 1.5 week in the field to study the catchment of the Hupsel Brook (a tributary of the Berkel).This field practical is an essential part of the BSc programme Soil, Water, Atmosphere.

However, due to the measures related to the Covid-19 outbreak we were forced to cancel the fieldwork in its usual form. Instead, students now do the field work close to home. They study a catchment in their neighbourhood, do experiments to determine infiltration capacity and hydraulic conductivity, they measure the water quality in their surroundings and they make daily weather observations.

A few of the results of those observations are available in the maps listed below. Most of the weather observations are made at 11 UTC.
